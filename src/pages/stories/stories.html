Impactful Stories

Why Giving Matters

Amy S. at Microsoft New England
Donated Kitchen Supplies This donation was given to a single mother of two young daughters who became homeless after ending an abusive domestic situation. The sudden loss of household income caused her to fall behind on rent, leading to her eviction. The mom turned to FamilyAid Boston, a nonprofit on Donii's platform. She was accepted into their Rapid Re-Housing program and was able to move into a new apartment with her girls. She is working with her social worker to maintain a budget, which does not allow for many household items. This generous donation of kitchen supplies was a much needed addition to her new home. Joanie W. at Microsoft New England
donated Women's Clothing Part of this donation was given to a single mother of an eight year-old son, working two jobs to provide a stable home for her family. She found herself homeless when she was forced to move out of her apartment due to a substantial rental increase. As the primary caregiver for her son and with no support services in place, she feared losing everything she worked so hard to accomplish. She and her son were placed in FamilyAid Boston’s emergency shelter program and soon found an apartment. The donation of good quality clothing has given her the confidence to seek a higher paying job, and she has said she now feels there is hope for her family’s future. Lauren C. at C-Space Donated Baby Items Through a charity she found on Donii, a donor named Lauren gave baby items and toys to a young single mother of a toddler and a premature newborn. The family lived on a shoestring budget. After a challenging pregnancy and continuing medical issues, Mom found herself unable to afford any baby essentials. Thanks to Lauren’s donation, the baby now has the basics she needs and her mom has much more peace of mind. Julia P. at C-Space Donated Women's Clothing When a single mother of three children was laid off from her full-time job and began to fall behind on her rent, she faced the prospect of being out on the streets with her children. After several unsuccessful attempts to obtain shelter for her family, she finally found assistance through FamilyAid Boston. The donation of clothing gave her the confidence she needed to apply for and get a higher paying job. She is now doing what she loves – working with children. Erica I. at C-Space Donated Kitchen Items A single mother and her two young sons were living in a motel with no kitchen and only a microwave. With the help of her social worker, the mother was able to get a better job and eventually move out of shelter into her own apartment. One of her greatest joys is cooking healthy meals for her two young boys. The gift of the new dining set made this possible and helped her new apartment feel like a home.
